Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar

The architect of modern India

Timeline of Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's Life:

  • 1891 - Born in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh, India into the “Untouchable” Mahar caste.
  • 1896 - Mother Bhimabai dies, and he was brought up by his aunt Meerabai until his father Ramji remarries.
  • 1907 - bearing lots of insult being an untouchable and constantly being mocked by his teachers, Ambedkar passes his matriculation exam.
  • 1912 - joins Elphinstone College for further education and completes Bachelor of Arts.
  • 1916 - receives a degree in Master of Arts and a Doctorate in Philosophy from Columbia University for his thesis on “National Dividend for India : A Historical and Analytical Study” and left to London for PhD but the Government of Baroda terminated his scholarship and recalled him to India and was appointed as a Military Secretary but no one would take orders from an “untouchable”. He could not even get lodging , not even the prime minister could help him find a place to live.
  • 1920 - returned to Bombay and started a fortnightly newspaper Mooknayak (Leader of the Dumb) in January and after accumulating sufficient funds, Ambedkar returned to London to complete his studies in September.
  • 1927 - receives PhD in Economics on thesis “Provincial Decentralization of Imperial Finance in British India” from London School of Economics and had decided to launch active movements against Untouchability. On December 25, he led thousands of followers to burn copies of Manusmriti (Laws of Manu).
  • 1929 - makes a controversial decision to co-operate with the all-British Simon Commission which was to look into setting up a responsible Indian Government in India.
  • 1935 - after reviewing the progress of his agitation against untouchability, he made a dramatic appeal to the “untouchables” encouraging them to forsake Hindu religion and convert to a religion where they would be treated with equality.
  • 1948 - The constituent of Assembly of Independent India appointed a Drafting committee with Dr. Ambedkar as its chairman to draft the Constitution if India.
  • 1949 - In 1948, Dr. Ambedkar presented the Draft Consititution before the people of India and was adopted on November 26, 1949 with all its 356 Articles and 8 Schedules.
  • 1956 - Dies at the age of 65.
